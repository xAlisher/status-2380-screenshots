Overall: Good flow with one persistent bug. The recovery phrase backup is well-structured — phrase is blurred by default, word verification is random and validated in real time, and the final choice (keep vs. delete from app) is safety-first. One clear bug: the red "Secure your recovery phrase" banner persists throughout the entire flow and doesn't clear until Done is tapped on the final screen.

01 — Home: Backup Banner

ElementRed persistent banner — "Secure your recovery phrase / Back up now"
Findings ✓ Banner is prominent and hard to dismiss accidentally (X requires deliberate tap)
✓ "Back up now" CTA is direct and actionable
⚠ Red colour = error/danger signal — appropriate for urgency but may feel alarming rather than guiding
⚠ No indication of how long the flow takes — users may defer thinking it's complex
SuggestionsAdd "Takes 2 minutes" next to the CTA to reduce deferral.

02 — Show Recovery Phrase

Element12 words blurred by default; "Reveal recovery phrase" button; warnings; "I've backed up phrase" (disabled until reveal)
Findings ✓ Words blurred by default — prevents shoulder-surfing
✓ "I've backed up phrase" disabled until user actively reveals — ensures deliberate action
✓ Two clear warnings: "never share" + "write it down" — plain language
✗ Red banner still showing at top — user is already in the backup flow, the nudge is redundant and confusing
⚠ No screenshot-blocking — sensitive screen should set FLAG_SECURE to prevent screen capture by other apps
Suggestions Dismiss/hide the "Secure your recovery phrase" banner once the user has entered this flow.
Add FLAG_SECURE (WindowManager) to prevent OS-level screenshot of the revealed phrase.

03 — Confirm Recovery Phrase

Element4 random word positions to fill in (1, 8, 10, 11); keyboard with BIP39 autocomplete suggestions
Findings ✓ Random word positions per session — prevents rote memorisation without understanding
✓ BIP39 word suggestions shown in keyboard bar — reduces typo errors
✗ Red banner still showing — user has been in the flow for 2 screens already
⚠ 4 of 12 words verified — industry standard, but sophisticated users may note this doesn't prove full backup
SuggestionsConsider confirming 6 words for stronger verification signal.

04 — Words Validated

ElementAll 4 fields showing green checkmarks; Continue enabled
Findings ✓ Real-time per-field validation with green checkmarks — clear feedback
✓ Continue only enabled when all fields are correct
⚠ Autocomplete suggestions don't update to match the correct word — shows unrelated words ("Escort, eskort, Escorta") while field is marked correct; minor visual noise

05 — Keep or Delete Phrase

ElementOption to permanently remove phrase from app; checkbox (opt-in to delete); Done button
Findings ✓ Destructive action (delete) is opt-in behind a checkbox — default is safe (keep)
✓ Clear consequence stated: "you will not be able to view it again"
⚠ No explanation of why a user would want to delete it — the tradeoff between security (remove attack surface) vs. convenience (view again later) isn't explained
SuggestionsAdd a one-line tooltip: "Removing it from the app reduces risk if your phone is compromised."

06 — Success

ElementRed banner gone; green toast — "You backed up your recovery phrase. Access it in Settings"
Findings ✓ Red banner clears immediately on flow completion — clean resolution
✓ Success toast tells user where to access the phrase again (Settings)
⚠ Toast auto-dismisses — users who miss it don't know where Settings access lives
SuggestionsMake the toast tappable (deep-link to Settings → Recovery phrase).
